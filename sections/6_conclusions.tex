\section{Conclusions and outlook}
\label{sec:conclusions}

\subsection{What was achieved}

The project closes a practical gap between raw video and shareable footage. \texttt{gdpr\_anonymizer} is a single public repository that reads an MP4 file, trains or applies a segmentation model locally, and writes a blurred MP4 while keeping datasets and weights off the remote \cite{RepoGDPR}. The Python extractor and Rust segmentator derive from upstream tooling \cite{DecrypheExtractor,DecrypheSegmentator}. The work reported here is the \texttt{ml\_pipeline} layer that connects labelling, training, validation, and blur into one repeatable command-line path instead of a chain of external tools.

To the author's knowledge, few open repositories cover that end-to-end path for both persons and vehicles in one place. Many strong alternatives are proprietary, costly, or limited to detection boxes rather than instance masks. Mask-based blur follows object contours and avoids the oversized rectangles that look poor on faces and plates in social-media video. Trials on short clips with a modest label set already produced convincing output. Further annotation should yield a stronger reusable checkpoint without altering the pipeline structure.

\subsection{Validation in practice}

The system works better than initially expected. Small labelled samples were enough to reach usable validation quality and acceptable blur on clips of several thousand frames. Performance still scales with data diversity and polygon accuracy, yet \texttt{gdpr-yolo-iterate} and error analysis make it possible to improve a model incrementally rather than begin again from scratch. Execution remains local throughout. A GPU shortens runtime but is not a prerequisite.

\subsection{Outlook}

The most visible limitation is temporal flicker when masks shift between consecutive frames. When stable segment coordinates appear in frames separated by a short gap, intermediate frames could inherit the same geometry before blur is applied. That change would fit the existing per-frame architecture.

Further gains depend chiefly on labelling: sharper polygons on difficult faces and plates, and broader training material across pedestrians, plate types, and countries. Assisted region selection in the segmentator would reduce manual effort and improve boundary precision. These steps would extend the current design rather than replace it.
